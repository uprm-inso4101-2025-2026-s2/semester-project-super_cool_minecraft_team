== Agile Development
Throughout the development of the Minecraft Mod Dependency Visualizer, Agile principles
have been present in the day-to-day rhythm of Team C's frontend work in ways that go beyond
theory. Each week, as team leader, I organize a Discord meeting with the frontend developers
to discuss the state of the project, propose new ideas, and define the issues that the team will
work on during that sprint. These issues are created incrementally and not all at once, because
the goal is to build the frontend page gradually, improving it week by week until reaching a
version that is complete and functional. This reflects one of the core ideas behind Agile:
delivering working software in small, consistent steps rather than attempting to build
everything in a single effort. Every Friday, the entire project participates in a Scrum-style
reflection posted on a GitHub Discussion, where each member writes what they worked on
during the week, what obstacles they ran into, and what their next steps are. This ritual keeps
the team accountable and makes progress visible to everyone, which is something Agile
heavily emphasizes through transparency and regular inspection. Beyond the internal team
dynamic, collaboration has also extended across groups. Meetings between the frontend and
backend sides of Team C have been necessary to make sure the interface connects properly
with what the backend is producing. Additionally, as frontend team leader, I have met with the
frontend leaders from the other teams, those responsible for the graph visualization and the
missing dependencies display, to align on how the different parts of the page fit together. This
kind of cross-team communication is exactly what Agile promotes, since a working product
depends not just on individual effort but on how well the pieces come together as a whole.
